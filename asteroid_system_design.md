# ASTEROID SYSTEM - Types, Sizes & Mineral Composition

## ASTEROID SIZES

Sizes determine mining duration, yield, and accessibility by ship class.

### TINY (40-60 units yield)
- **Mining Time**: 1.5 seconds
- **Difficulty**: Easiest, almost no risk
- **Hold Space**: 40-60 units
- **Accessible By**: All ships
- **Composition**: Single dominant element (80%+), minimal variety
- **Purpose**: Tutorial/warm-up, quick credits, beginner farming
- **Visual**: ~80px diameter in-game

### SMALL (80-120 units yield)
- **Mining Time**: 2.5 seconds
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Hold Space**: 80-120 units
- **Accessible By**: Scout+ (all ships)
- **Composition**: 2-3 dominant elements
- **Purpose**: Early game grinding, reliable income
- **Visual**: ~120px diameter in-game

### MEDIUM (150-220 units yield)
- **Mining Time**: 3.5 seconds
- **Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Hold Space**: 150-220 units
- **Accessible By**: Prospector+ (not Scout)
- **Composition**: 4-6 elements, good variety
- **Purpose**: Mid-game farming, mixed yields
- **Visual**: ~160px diameter in-game

### LARGE (280-400 units yield)
- **Mining Time**: 5 seconds
- **Difficulty**: Challenging, power-intensive
- **Hold Space**: 280-400 units
- **Accessible By**: Harvester+ (not Scout/Prospector)
- **Composition**: 6-9 elements, highest variety
- **Purpose**: Major credit hauls, rare element concentration
- **Visual**: ~200px diameter in-game

### MASSIVE (500-700 units yield)
- **Mining Time**: 7 seconds
- **Difficulty**: Very challenging, extreme power drain
- **Hold Space**: 500-700 units
- **Accessible By**: Industrial+ (not Scout/Prospector/Harvester)
- **Composition**: All 9-12 elements including rare variants
- **Purpose**: Endgame grinding, guaranteed rare yields
- **Visual**: ~250px diameter in-game
- **Special**: 10% chance of exotic elements

### COLOSSAL (1000+ units yield, ultra-rare)
- **Mining Time**: 10 seconds
- **Difficulty**: Extreme, nearly impossible alone
- **Hold Space**: 1000+ units
- **Accessible By**: Titan only
- **Composition**: Full elemental suite + guaranteed exotics
- **Purpose**: True endgame, alliance/strategy required
- **Visual**: ~300px diameter in-game
- **Special**: Always contains exotic elements, 5% chance of legendary materials
- **Spawn Rate**: 1 in 200 scans

## ASTEROID TYPES

Beyond size, asteroids have **composition types** that determine what minerals are most abundant. Each type has realistic mineralogy.

### TYPE A: IRON-NICKEL (Siderite Meteorites)
**Composition Profile**:
- Fe: 88-92% (Iron)
- Ni: 5-8% (Nickel)
- Co: 1-2% (Cobalt)
- Trace: <1% (other metals)

**Characteristics**:
- Most common asteroid type (40% spawn rate)
- **Value Profile**: High volume, moderate value
- **Best For**: Beginners, reliable income, iron farming
- **Mining Bonus**: Standard yield
- **Price Premium**: None (common metals)
- **Visual**: Dark metallic gray with metallic sheen

**Size Variant - TINY**:
- Fe: 90%, Ni: 8%, Co: 2%
- Yield: 50 units Fe (45), 4 Ni, 1 Co

**Size Variant - SMALL**:
- Fe: 90%, Ni: 7%, Co: 3%
- Yield: 95 Fe, 7 Ni, 3 Co (~105 units)

**Size Variant - MEDIUM**:
- Fe: 89%, Ni: 8%, Co: 3%
- Yield: 178 Fe, 16 Ni, 6 Co (~200 units)

**Size Variant - LARGE**:
- Fe: 88%, Ni: 9%, Co: 3%
- Yield: 330 Fe, 34 Ni, 9 Co (~373 units)

**Size Variant - MASSIVE**:
- Fe: 89%, Ni: 8%, Co: 3%
- Yield: 535 Fe, 48 Ni, 18 Co (~601 units)

---

### TYPE B: CARBONACEOUS (Chondrite Meteorites)
**Composition Profile**:
- O: 35-40% (Oxygen, in silicates)
- Si: 18-22% (Silicon, in silicates)
- Mg: 12-15% (Magnesium, in silicates)
- Fe: 8-12% (Iron, in minerals)
- S: 3-5% (Sulfur)
- Ni: 1-2% (Nickel)
- Trace: Carbon, water, organic compounds

**Characteristics**:
- Second most common (35% spawn rate)
- **Value Profile**: Medium volume, moderate-high value (silicates + sulfur)
- **Best For**: Mid-game diversity, elemental variety
- **Mining Bonus**: Slight yield boost for rare elements (+5%)
- **Price Premium**: Higher sulfur/magnesium prices
- **Visual**: Dark gray-brown with dusty texture

**Size Variant - TINY**:
- O: 38%, Si: 20%, Mg: 14%, Fe: 10%, S: 4%, Ni: 14%
- Yield: Mixed 50-60 units

**Size Variant - SMALL**:
- O: 37%, Si: 20%, Mg: 15%, Fe: 11%, S: 4%, Ni: 13%
- Yield: ~110 units mixed

**Size Variant - MEDIUM**:
- O: 38%, Si: 19%, Mg: 14%, Fe: 10%, S: 5%, Ni: 14%
- Yield: ~200 units mixed

**Size Variant - LARGE**:
- O: 36%, Si: 21%, Mg: 15%, Fe: 12%, S: 4%, Ni: 12%
- Yield: ~370 units mixed

**Size Variant - MASSIVE**:
- O: 37%, Si: 20%, Mg: 14%, Fe: 11%, S: 5%, Ni: 13%
- Yield: ~600 units mixed

---

### TYPE C: RARE-EARTH (Specialty - Premium Asteroids)
**Composition Profile**:
- Fe: 25-30%
- Ni: 8-12% (concentrated)
- O: 20-25%
- Si: 10-12%
- Co: 3-5% (HIGH - rare element concentration)
- Cr: 2-4% (HIGH - rare element concentration)
- Mn: 1-3% (HIGH - rare element concentration)
- Mg: 3-5%
- S: 2-3%

**Characteristics**:
- Uncommon (20% spawn rate)
- **Value Profile**: Lower volume, MUCH higher value (rare elements concentrated)
- **Best For**: Late-game farming, rare element hunting
- **Mining Bonus**: +20% rare element yield when mined
- **Price Premium**: Extremely high (Co, Cr, Mn are 3-4x more valuable)
- **Special**: Void Resonator tool works best on these
- **Visual**: Shimmering dark with metallic flecks, distinctive appearance

**Size Variant - SMALL**:
- Rare Elements Concentrated: Co 4%, Cr 3%, Mn 2%
- Yield: ~100 units, ~8 Co, ~3 Cr, ~2 Mn (ultra-valuable)

**Size Variant - MEDIUM**:
- Yield: ~200 units, ~16 Co, ~6 Cr, ~4 Mn

**Size Variant - LARGE**:
- Yield: ~370 units, ~30 Co, ~12 Cr, ~8 Mn

**Size Variant - MASSIVE**:
- Yield: ~600 units, ~48 Co, ~20 Cr, ~12 Mn

---

### TYPE D: OLIVINE-RICH (Specialty - Bulk Silicate)
**Composition Profile**:
- O: 45-50% (very high, in olivine silicate)
- Si: 25-30% (very high, in olivine silicate)
- Mg: 15-20% (moderately high)
- Fe: 3-5% (lower, in olivine)
- Ni: 1-2%

**Characteristics**:
- Uncommon (5% spawn rate)
- **Value Profile**: HUGE volume, modest value (silicates are bulk commodity)
- **Best For**: Pure farming for bulk materials, volume grinding
- **Mining Bonus**: +30% volume yield
- **Price Premium**: None (common silicates)
- **Visual**: Olive-green/brown tinted, shiny

**Size Variant - MEDIUM**:
- Yield: ~250 units (high volume)
- Primarily O and Si (~225 units O+Si, ~25 Mg)

**Size Variant - LARGE**:
- Yield: ~480 units (massive volume)

**Size Variant - MASSIVE**:
- Yield: ~800+ units (exceptional for volume farmers)

## ASTEROID SPAWN PROBABILITIES

When scanning, the game picks:
1. **Size** (weighted by ship class)
2. **Type** (weighted toward common types)
3. **Composition variation** (random variance within type)

### Scout Class (Small asteroids only)
- Tiny: 30%
- Small: 70%
- Types: Iron-Nickel 60%, Carbonaceous 40%

### Prospector Class (Small-Medium)
- Tiny: 10%
- Small: 60%
- Medium: 30%
- Types: Iron-Nickel 45%, Carbonaceous 40%, Rare-Earth 15%

### Harvester Class (All asteroids)
- Tiny: 5%
- Small: 40%
- Medium: 40%
- Large: 15%
- Types: Iron-Nickel 35%, Carbonaceous 35%, Rare-Earth 20%, Olivine 10%

### Industrial Class (All asteroids, better spawns)
- Small: 10%
- Medium: 35%
- Large: 40%
- Massive: 15%
- Types: Iron-Nickel 30%, Carbonaceous 30%, Rare-Earth 25%, Olivine 15%

### Titan Class (Best spawns, rarest included)
- Medium: 5%
- Large: 30%
- Massive: 55%
- Colossal: 10% (exclusive to Titan)
- Types: Iron-Nickel 20%, Carbonaceous 25%, Rare-Earth 35%, Olivine 15%, Exotic 5%

## SPECIAL ASTEROID PHENOMENA

### METALLIC SHEATH (Coating Effect)
- 10% of all asteroids have a metallic coating
- **Effect**: First 20% of mining yields +50% rare elements, then returns to normal
- **Purpose**: Rewards speed, punishes slow mining
- **Visual**: Shiny metallic surface that dulls during mining

### FRACTURED CORE (Weakness Mechanic)
- 5% of large/massive asteroids are internally fractured
- **Effect**: Mining time reduced by 40%, but 15% chance of material loss
- **Purpose**: High-risk/high-reward variant
- **Visual**: Visible cracks/fractures on surface

### UNSTABLE DECAY
- 3% of asteroids (rare-earth types only)
- **Effect**: Yields rare elements in 5x concentration for 2 mining cycles, then asteroid "decays" (disappears)
- **Purpose**: Time-pressure gameplay, forces decision-making
- **Visual**: Glowing/pulsing surface

## PROGRESSION STRATEGY

**Early Game (Scout Class)**
- Farm Iron-Nickel Tiny/Small asteroids
- Build credit base quickly (reliable yield)
- Avoid Rare-Earth (requires better tools to maximize value)
- Goal: 2,000 credits for Prospector upgrade

**Mid Game (Prospector → Harvester)**
- Mix Iron-Nickel and Carbonaceous types
- Start hunting Rare-Earth small/medium when tools improve
- Olivine asteroids for volume when hold is empty
- Goal: 25,000 credits for Industrial upgrade

**Late Game (Industrial → Titan)**
- Focus heavily on Rare-Earth asteroids (35-40% yield value)
- Large asteroids for maximum per-cycle value
- Special phenomena (Metallic Sheath, Unstable Decay) for optimization
- Goal: Maximize credits/hour

**Endgame (Titan)**
- Farm Massive/Colossal asteroids exclusively
- Hunt for Exotic element variants (new materials with unique properties)
- Collect all element types for "completion"

## DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

**Simplicity with Depth**: Five asteroid types seem like a lot, but each fulfills a role:
- Iron-Nickel: Beginner-friendly, consistent
- Carbonaceous: Diversity, intermediate value
- Rare-Earth: Target for advanced players hunting value
- Olivine: Pure volume farming
- (Future) Exotic: Endgame collection/mystery

**Natural Progression**: Players naturally encounter types as they upgrade ships and tools. Scout players won't see Rare-Earth often, but Titan players will.

**Meaningful Choices**: "Do I mine this small Rare-Earth or wait for a large Iron-Nickel?" Different types reward different strategies.

**Realistic Grounding**: All types are real meteorite categories (chondrites, siderites, carbonaceous chondrites). Compositions are scientifically plausible.
